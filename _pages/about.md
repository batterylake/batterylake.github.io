I'm broadly interested in how we can assist non-technical decision makers in high-stakes scenarios with data-intensive methods. My prior work has explored this question in the context of social media and defense.

Highlights
======
- (Mar 2024) Our paper [Defending novice user privacy: An evaluation of default web browser configurations](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0167404824000853) was published in Computers & Security.
- (Feb 2024) Our paper [LLMs Among Us: Generative AI Participating in Digital Discourse](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.07940) was accepted to AAAI-MAKE 2024. Our results were featured in [Notre Dame News](https://news.nd.edu/news/ai-among-us-social-media-users-struggle-to-identify-ai-bots-during-political-discourse/) and [Futurity](https://www.futurity.org/social-media-users-ai-bots-political-discourse-3185632/)
- (May 2023) I completed my Bachelor of Science at the University of Notre Dame in Mathematics (Honors) and Philosophy. I then joined the ND Center for Research Computing as a Research Fellow.
