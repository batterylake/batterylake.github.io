[Preventing an AI-related catastrophe](https://80000hours.org/problem-profiles/artificial-intelligence/#is-this-a-form-of-pascals-mugging-taking-a-big-bet-on-tiny-probabilities)    
Benjamin Hilton Aug 2022  

TL;DR  
Hilton advocates for AI risk-reducation as a cause area warranting greater attention by demonstrating the risks posed by highly intelligent AI systems  

The argument can be modeled as follows:  

1. Catastrophic risk relating to AI is one of the most pressing problems in the world  
    1. We will build AI systems with [instrumental rationality](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/rationality-instrumental/)  
	  2. The interests of such systems may diverge from that of humanity  
	  3. Disempowerment by AI systems would be an existential catastrophe  
	  4. There are incentives to deploy misaligned AI systems, despite this risk  
2. Research is underfunded and understaffed ($50m in 2020, 400 FTE)
3. There are multiple avenues for contribution including technical, strategy, and policy research, operations, and journalism

Some intersting data points mentioned include:  
- Surveys indicate most AI reserachers publishing at top conferences assign greater than a 5% likelihood of an existential catastrophe casused by AI  
- Compute is easy to scale, with the largest ML models using 1 billion times more compute than in 2012  
- According to a survey of 300 AI experts in 2019, the probability of creating AGI is 20% by 2036, 50% by 2060, and 85% by 2100  

Thoughts  
This is an excellent resource to share with friends or family explaing why we should care about reducing risk stemming from AI. ALthough, I suspect in light of recent advancements such as ChatGPT, many people outside tech or EA will be friendly to the idea of AI safety research.
